5 Ways to Keep Your Indoor Cat Active

schedule

5 min read

Published Oct 24, 2023

While indoor cats are safer from outdoor hazards, they can easily become bored and sedentary. Keeping your feline friend physically active and mentally stimulated is crucial for their long-term health.

vertical_align_top

1. Utilize Vertical Space

Cats naturally love to climb and observe their surroundings from above. Providing vertical territory increases their living space and encourages movement.

  • Install wall-mounted cat shelves.
  • Invest in a multi-level cat tree.
  • Clear off the tops of stable bookshelves or cabinets.
sports_esports

2. Interactive Play Sessions

Interactive play mimics the hunting experience. Dedicate at least 15 minutes twice a day for focused play using wand toys or laser pointers.

"Pro tip: Always end a laser pointer session by letting your cat 'catch' a physical toy or treat to avoid frustration."
extension

3. Food Puzzles & Foraging

Don't just put food in a bowl. Make them work for it! Food puzzles slow down fast eaters and engage their problem-solving skills.

  • Hide dry treats around the house for them to find.
  • Use rolling treat dispensers.
  • DIY puzzles using egg cartons or toilet paper rolls.
window

4. Enhance Window Views

"Cat TV" is real. A window overlooking a bird feeder or a busy street provides hours of visual stimulation.

  • Place a perch or "cat hammock" by the sunniest window.
  • Install a bird feeder outside within view.
  • Keep blinds open during the day.
ads_click

5. Clicker Training

Believe it or not, cats can be trained! Clicker training is an excellent way to bond and provide intense mental exercise.

Start with simple commands like 'sit' or 'high five' using a clicker and high-value treats. It keeps their minds sharp and bodies engaged.
